What Is Food Coherence?

Food Coherence represents a novel clinical and diagnostic measure of how food impacts biological integrity. It quantifies systemic stress, mitochondrial resilience, and regenerative pattern fidelity based on lab-detectable electrical, redox, and inflammatory signals. This score bridges public health, medicine, and regulatory safety gaps currently left unaddressed by traditional nutrition labels.

The Food Coherence Label

Products receive a 0–100 score indicating bioelectrical coherence and chronic burden. This label supports front-of-pack guidance, institutional procurement standards, and EU legal compliance under Article 14 (long-term safety).

  • 80–100: High Coherence – restorative to cellular signaling
  • 50–79: Moderate Coherence – conditionally safe
  • 0–49: Low Coherence – chronic immune/inflammatory signal risk

The Bioelectric Impact of Food

Scientific evidence supports that food acts as an instructional signal. Ultra-processed foods induce electrical chaos: depolarizing membranes, suppressing mitochondrial voltage (ΔΨm), and sustaining chronic immune activation. These disruptions precede clinical disease and are measurable through optical, electrical, and redox metrics.

Legal Alignment

The Food Coherence system fulfills legal gaps in food safety regulation, particularly in the EU:

  • Article 14: Covers chronic toxicity and physiological dysfunction, not just acute harm.
  • Article 2: Redefines food as a systemic exposure, not a product unit.
  • Article 16: Prohibits misleading omissions — absence of coherence data now qualifies.
