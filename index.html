What Is Food Coherence?

Food Coherence measures the structural and energetic integrity of foods after processing, using lab signals such as mitochondrial voltage, tissue memory, and electrical coherence. It indicates how well food supports or disrupts biological signaling and tissue health over time.

The Food Coherence Label

  • 80–100: High Coherence (minimally processed)
  • 50–79: Moderate Coherence
  • 0–49: Low Coherence (ultra-processed)

Bioelectric Science

Ultra-processed foods destabilize cellular potentials, reduce mitochondrial voltage, and suppress pattern memory. Coherent foods preserve these systems, helping prevent chronic disease from the bioelectric level up.

Legal Framework

This initiative fulfills key unmet obligations of EU Regulation EC 178/2002, including cumulative risk, chronic stress, and label transparency.

  • Art. 2: Food as system exposure
  • Art. 14: Unsafe includes long-term functional harm
  • Art. 16: Labels must not mislead by omission
